8.Rail – Schiene

On December 1, 1838 a steam train travelled the 12 kilometres route from Brunswick to Wolfenbüttel for the first time. In its early years this first German state railway served mainly to carry people on day-trips and for market traffic. Railway travel became an attraction in the Duchy of Brunswick. The trains with the steam locomotives “Brunswick” and “Lion” ran four times daily between Brunswick and Wolfenbüttel. More than 100,000 travellers were registered in the first five months. Not until after 1841 did the railway begin to carry freight. The extension of the line followed.
